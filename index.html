About Us

Cryptic Languages

My passion for STEM started young, at seven years old. I was fortunate enough to have a family that supported my pursuits, and my passion for STEM was fostered in a supportive environment that allowed it to grow overtime. Unfortunately, some girls and women don’t have the opportunity to learn if they are passionate about STEM, as they are discouraged from pursuing it at a young age. I have witnessed this with my own eyes, as male students overtook robotics and engineering projects at school, while female students were left with doing inventory work.

One of the reasons there is a lack of females in STEM is because of the toxic learning environments in classrooms. To address this issue, I created Cryptic Languages. Using this website, girls can learn to code in the environment of their choice, and will not be put down by other students in toxic learning environments. The website also features resources such as podcasts and articles, so our users can learn more about the positive and negative of technology. Feel free to explore all of the resources available on this site, and share this website with others hoping to learn to code!

Learn more about Cryptic Languages by taking a look at our one pager and partnership package

Ramandeep Saini
